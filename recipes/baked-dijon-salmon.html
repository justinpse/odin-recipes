Baked Dijon Salmon

This mustard-crusted salmon is a wonderful way to prepare fresh salmon fillets in the oven. Be sure to make extra â€” your family will be begging for more!

Buckle up, this recipe is way longer than the first one! But it'll be well worth it. Takes a little longer than Tuna Fish Salads, but boy it'll fill you up!

Personally, I love me some salmon. I hope you'll like this recipe as much as I (and many others) do!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 3 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 1/2 tbsp honey
  • 1/4 cup dry bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped pecans
  • 4 tsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 4 (4 oz) fillets salmon
  • Sald and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 200 C.
  2. Stir together butter, mustard, and honey in a small bowl. Set aside.
  3. Mix together bread crumbs, pecans, and parsley in another bowl.
  4. Brush each salmon fillet lightly with honey mustard mixture.
  5. Sprinkle the topos of fillets with bread crumb mixture.
  6. Bake salmon in the preheated oven until it flakes easily with a fork, 12 to 15 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper
  8. Garnish with a wedge of lemon if you wish!
  9. And you're done! Get serving and get eating!
