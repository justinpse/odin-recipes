Famous Chicken Adobo

Classic chicken adobo recipe that's simple to make and loved by all who try it. It has been modified to be a bit saucier than traditional adobo and is delicious served over rice.

This Filipino classic is a loved recipe for sure. Practically a staple in Filipino homes! With this recipe, your mouth will surely start watering.

Feel free to serve this with plain rice, or fried rice. Hell, you can even eat it on it's own without anything else! Go crazy.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 lb) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 2/3 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp garclic powder
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Directions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skiller over medium-high heat. Cook chicken until golden brown, 2-3 minutes per side. Transfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Add onion and garlic to skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes.
  3. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  4. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35-40 minutes.
  6. Serve with rice or fried rice. Enjoy!
